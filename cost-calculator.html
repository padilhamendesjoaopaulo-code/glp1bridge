How GLP-1 costs work under Medicare

Under Medicare Part D, your cost moves through phases — a deductible, an initial coverage period, and (as of 2025) an annual out-of-pocket cap established by the Inflation Reduction Act. Once you hit that cap, you pay $0 for covered drugs for the rest of the year. The Bridge program is designed to bring qualifying GLP-1 prescriptions under this Part D umbrella so the cap applies.

  • Cash price for brand GLP-1s commonly runs ~$1,000–$1,350/month without coverage.
  • Manufacturer savings cards generally exclude people with Medicare or other government coverage.
  • Bridge / Part D coverage can dramatically lower your share once prior authorization is approved.

Sources: CMS.gov Part D cost phases and the Medicare prescription drug out-of-pocket cap.
